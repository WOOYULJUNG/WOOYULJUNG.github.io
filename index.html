I am an MS student in Artificial Intelligence at Seoul National University, where I am advised by Prof. Byoung-Tak Zhang. I am currently a visiting research student at the University of Toronto, and I also collaborate with LG Electronics’ Toronto AI Lab. I received my B.S. in Electronic Engineering from Hanyang University in 2025.

My long-term goal is to understand how physically grounded algorithms in the brain can inspire artificial intelligence. I am broadly interested in the space between neuroscience, physics, and machine learning, and in bringing ideas from neural dynamics into AI systems.

I want to test these ideas through action generation in interactive environments, especially in settings involving control and robotic manipulation. In that context, I am particularly fascinated by the notion of attractors as a unifying language for memory, computation, and decision-making.

Research Interests

  • Brain-Inspired Learning & Physical Algorithms: biologically and physically grounded principles for learning and computation
  • Neural Dynamics & Associative Memory: attractor dynamics, Hopfield and modern Hopfield models, and dynamical systems analysis of recurrent networks
  • Action, Control & Robotics: recurrent dynamics for working memory, sequential decision-making, motor planning, and robotic manipulation

Selected Publications

  1. Deep Neural Networks as Finite-step Hopfield Dynamics
    Wooyul Jung, B. Hochan, Y. Heebin, and Byoung-Tak Zhang
    In ICLR Workshop on New Frontiers in Associative Memories (NFAM), 2026
    We establish a formal connection between the forward pass of deep neural networks and finite-step retrieval dynamics in modern Hopfield networks, providing a unified energy-based view of deep representation learning.
    PDF Code Poster
  2. Echo State Networks for Efficient Imitation Learning in Robotic Manipulation
    Y. Joo, S. Choi, Wooyul Jung, and Byoung-Tak Zhang
    In AAAI Workshop on NeuroAI, 2026
    We propose using echo state networks (ESNs) as efficient recurrent modules for imitation learning in robotic manipulation, demonstrating that reservoir computing dynamics can match or exceed trained RNNs with a fraction of the parameters.
    PDF Poster
  3. Shaping Latent Geometry with Noise-Injected Hopfield Dynamics
    Wooyul Jung, Y. Joo, D. Yu, S. Choi, and Byoung-Tak Zhang
    In NeurIPS Workshop on Symmetry and Geometry in Neural Representations (NeurReps), 2025
    We show that injecting structured noise into Hopfield retrieval dynamics systematically reshapes the geometry of learned representations, inducing disentanglement and symmetry-aligned latent structure aligned with downstream task requirements.
    PDF Poster
  4. Modern Hopfield Networks with Latent-Space Retrieval
    Wooyul Jung, Y. Joo, D. Yu, and Byoung-Tak Zhang
    In Korea Software Congress (KSC), 2025
    Best Paper Award (Top 1%)
    We extend modern Hopfield networks to perform associative retrieval in a compressed latent space, improving memory capacity and retrieval quality while reducing computational cost.
